The audience is researchers, academics, and practitioners who are designing their own training — they are NOT trained in pedagogy. Steps must be concrete and actionable for this audience.

Check for the following issues:

1. **Correctness** — Do the steps accurately reflect what a training designer would need to do? Are any steps misleading or wrong?
2. **Completeness** — Is anything missing that the reader would need to actually follow through? Would someone get stuck between any two steps?
3. **Clarity** — Could a first-time reader follow each step without guessing? Are instructions specific enough, or do they rely on jargon or assumed knowledge?
4. **Order** — Are the steps in a logical sequence? Does each step build naturally on the previous one?
5. **Consistency** — If there are steps in both "Practical guidance" and "Suggested process", do they align with each other? They don't need to be identical, but they should not contradict.
